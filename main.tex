\begin{abstract}
We propose a generative framework for right-censored survival data based on
two conditional normalizing flows that jointly model the failure time
$T \mid Z$ and the censoring time $C \mid Z$. Each flow is a strictly monotone
transform on the log-time axis with a Gaussian base, yielding closed-form
density and survival functions and a fully tractable censored joint
log-likelihood. We additionally introduce a differentiable
Soft--Nelson--Aalen Wasserstein regularizer that aligns the model-implied
population survival curve with the empirical one, yielding a sample-level
likelihood objective that is augmented with a population-level distributional
constraint. The flow construction is sufficiently expressive to capture
non-LogNormal hazard shapes (Cox-PH, heavy-tailed) where parametric
alternatives provably fail; on a four-scenario simulation study with $30\%$ to
$77\%$ censoring, the proposed model improves T-side Wasserstein-1 by up to
$32\%$ and Kolmogorov--Smirnov by up to $66\%$ over Kaplan--Meier, Cox PH,
parametric AFT, and a parametric LogNormal-flow ablation.
\end{abstract}
