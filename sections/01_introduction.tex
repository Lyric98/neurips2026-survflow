\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item A conditional normalizing flow for jointly modeling event time
          $T$ and censoring time $C$ under right censoring, with
          closed-form density, survival function, and censored
          log-likelihood. The flow extends to informative censoring via a
          shared-latent variable that induces $T$-$C$ dependence without
          specifying a copula family.
    \item A differentiable Soft--Nelson--Aalen Wasserstein regularizer
          that augments the censored MLE with a population-level
          distributional constraint, providing scenario-dependent
          improvement (up to $10\%$ KS reduction on Cox-PH DGPs).
    \item An identifiability result showing that the marginal distribution
          of $T$ is identified from right-censored data under the proposed
          flow class, extending the Czado--Van~Keilegom framework to
          normalizing flows.
    \item Comprehensive evaluation on five synthetic DGPs under both
          uninformative and informative censoring (two distinct frailty
          mechanisms, $\rho \in \{0, 0.5, 1\}$), and on the Rotterdam
          breast cancer dataset ($n = 2{,}982$, $d = 7$). The flow is the
          only method competitive across all settings: it matches or beats
          correctly-specified parametric models and T-only deep baselines,
          and maintains accuracy under informative censoring where
          independence-assuming methods degrade.
\end{itemize}
